Corruption: A Way to Happiness

Written October 28, 2020

Undeniably, there are several people who don’t earn enough to provide for the needs of their families. In this world of cruelty and despair, many lose hope and stop searching for solutions. Regrettably yet inevitably, some people would prefer to succumb to death than live in this world any longer. However, these people need only enlightenment to improve their way of life; and I, if I may humbly do so, would like to propose a solution to our country’s current predicament.

There are many opportunities and possibilities in life. Solutions to improve one’s economic welfare are abounding. For instance, you can turn your hobbies into your source of income. However, rather than committing oneself to arduous tasks, only one answer is certified to bring us wealth almost instantly, and it would be none other than our perennial friend, corruption.

Since forever, corruption has been present in our world; and during its lifetime, it has garnered the hate and criticism of most. Until now, I had not an inkling as to why people treat it as something atrocious when it is most logical and justifiable. For those of you who are skeptics of my ideals, it would be my honor to show you that corruption is an ideal path to success so as to expand your mind even in the slightest sliver.

Throughout the years, many intellectuals have believed that human nature would do anything to fulfill their desires. According to Thomas Hobbes, a knowledgeable philosopher, humans would carry out any action to aid their survival even though it may harm someone else. The loathsome responses towards corruption only bewilder me more. In corporations and daily life, it is common for people to drag their opponents down, a phenomenon called “The Crab Mentality.” For the benefit of oneself, this is not an unusual occurrence. Since raking away some funds from the country would be in the same category as the Crab Mentality, corruption should be normalized to alleviate the desires growing within the hearts of our people in our society.

Another reason as to why corruption is an abomination to most is because it brings detrimental effects to our country’s economy. Corruption hinders economic growth and causes an uneven distribution of wealth. Nevertheless, instead of being the one affected by corruption, be the corruptor. Although it may sound impossible and intimidating, think of the many benefits corruption may give and you’ll be given enough inspiration to continue down this path. Looking upon the role models in the history of corruption would also provide assistance and guidance toward your goal.

There are many people in history who have committed corruption, and they always get away with it in the end. For instance, a recent case of corruption would be by Bong Revilla. He was charged for plunder after being involved in the well-known pork barrel scam case. After being imprisoned for four years, Sandiganbayan decides to acquit him of his crimes because of the “lack of quantum evidence.” He even returns to work again for the government, which shows his perseverance and his fortitude to forge the path of corruption once again.

Another example would be Ferdinand E. Marcos, the most capable president in Philippine history. He spent his reign as the president taking full control of our country through martial law and spending his life full of luxury by stealing from the Philippine budget and loaning some money from the world bank. After he was ousted from his position because of the utterly nonsensical People Power Revolution, he and his wife were charged with graft and premeditated murder. He was supposed to receive the death penalty, yet instead, he was exiled into Honolulu, Hawaii. There he and his beloved wife lived a comfortable life of lavish luxury. They stayed in a multi-million-dollar home, dined in high-class restaurants every day, and shopped for designer clothes. In exchange for imprisonment, they get to experience this life until their last breath. With all honesty, wouldn’t you want to do the same?

If you get caught and charged for your crime by chance, it isn’t the end for your thieving legacy. After a few years have passed and memories of the crime have simmered down, corrupt officials have the audacity to run for upcoming elections. The surprising thing about this is that they win, all because of their swift name recognition after being mentioned by the news several times. As mentioned, an example would be Bong Revilla, who is currently a senator working for the government.

Corruption is an admirable act, and merely reading this mellifluous word gives warmth to my heart. It emancipates the hidden desires of people, liberating us from the burden of unfollowed dreams. It is my hope that the world would continue this legacy onto the following generations to let them have a taste of what it feels to be alive.
